Overview


Air pollution causes an estimated 7.9 million premature deaths annually, making accurate forecasting a critical public health priority. Machine learning is increasingly being applied to forecast air pollution levels, yet existing benchmarks remain narrow in both geographic scope and pollutant coverage, and fail to evaluate the latest generation of time series foundation models (TSFMs) on real-world, large-scale data.

To bridge this gap, we introduce AQA-Bench, a large-scale multi-country and multi-pollutant dataset and benchmark. AQA-Bench covers 6 major pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, CO, and O3) over a three-year period (July 2022 - June 2025) across 7 diverse countries and 4 continents, compiling more than 14,000 station-pollutant series.

We benchmark this dataset across 11 leading time series foundation models and 6 classical baselines to evaluate performance on short-term air quality forecasting. Our framework directly tests whether pretrained zero-shot foundation models can successfully compete with localized models optimized specifically on target stream metrics.

Dataset Curation

Real-World Datasets: Raw granular observations were curated directly from 6 primary regulatory networks: EPA (US), CPCB (India), AURN (UK), CNEMC (China), EEA (France/Germany), and SINAICA (Mexico).

Benchmark Design

Evaluation Protocol

1. Strict Continuity Preprocessing: Monitored streams are constrained to locations retaining at least 70% data integrity with zero gaps longer than two weeks. Missing temporal points are resolved via state-of-the-art Multiple Seasonal-Trend decomposition using LOESS (MSTL).

2. Standardized Task Setup: Formulated in a strictly univariate setting using a 168-hour context window to predict a rolling 24-hour target timeline.

Key Findings

Finding 1. Multi-modal Cross-Architectures Lead the Standings.
VisionTS++ achieves the top validation metric worldwide by translating temporal charts into visual arrays and analyzing them utilizing a vision foundation encoder. This highlights the potency of cross-modal pretraining rules over isolated step processing.

Finding 2. Zero-Shot TSFMs Consistently Beat Local Track Customizations.
Pretrained foundation systems operated out-of-the-box without localized adaptation consistently outperform traditional statistical rules and specialized machine learning baselines (like LightGBM and DLinear) optimized parameters directly on targeted target test regions.

Finding 3. Distributional Severity Demands Global Benchmark Diversity.
Evaluating frameworks solely on clean Western atmospheres masks model behavior in highly complex, high-emission sectors. On challenging networks like India's CPCB, traditional deep learning networks fail to clear the basic threshold set by Seasonal Naive baselines.
